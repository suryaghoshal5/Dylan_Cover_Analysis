The Canon

Five Songs That Became a Movement

Between 1962 and 1964, Bob Dylan wrote a handful of songs that would define the protest genre for the next six decades. These five were covered more than any others — and they kept being covered long after the folk revival ended, resurfacing whenever the political temperature rose.

Song by Song

Each Protest Song Has Its Own Rhythm

Not all protest songs resurface at the same moments. "Blowin' in the Wind" is the universal anthem — covered in every era. "Masters of War" spikes around actual wars. "The Times They Are A-Changin'" tracks generational transitions. Each song has become a specific kind of political instrument.

Cover frequency broken out by song. "Blowin' in the Wind" dominates across all eras; "Masters of War" shows sharper, more event-driven spikes.

By Political Era

The Covers Mirror Their Time

Each political era has a different relationship with Dylan's protest songs. Vietnam-era covers carry folk sincerity; post-9/11 versions are rawer and more urgent; the BLM/COVID era sees the widest genre diversity yet.

Number of Spotify-matched covers per song per political era. Darker cells indicate more covers. "Blowin' in the Wind" is consistently the most covered across all eras.

Staying Power

Do Protest Covers From Turbulent Eras Last?

Spotify popularity is a proxy for whether a cover endured beyond its political moment. Vietnam-era covers have the highest median popularity — they've had decades to accumulate cultural weight. Recent BLM/COVID covers show early streaming traction but haven't yet proven their staying power.

The Protest Song Never Retires

64 years after "Blowin' in the Wind," Dylan's protest songs are still being recorded — not as nostalgia, but as active political tools. Each generation finds new urgency in the same words, new sound in the same chords. The songs don't belong to the sixties. They belong to whenever things get bad enough that someone picks up a guitar and starts again.

“The answer, my friend, is blowin' in the wind.” — Still being sung, 2025
